# Supplementary Figure Legends

A mechanistic catalogue of 26 methods and an evidence framework for interpreting single-cell dimensionality reduction

**Supplementary Fig. S1 | Dataset structure and analysis context for claim-specific embedding interpretation.** a, Locked dataset scale for PBMC3k, Paul15 and the heart-atlas subset. Points show cells and genes in each processed analysis object; point size denotes the number of label levels used by the diagnostic framework. b, Label-burden distribution for the three empirical contexts. Each point is a label level; the dashed line marks the rare-state threshold used to flag low-abundance states. c, Donor-level cell-count distribution in the heart-atlas subset, showing why donor-aware diagnostics are required for tissue-organisation claims. d, Label-burden metrics across datasets, including imbalance, largest-label fraction, rare-cell fraction, rare-label fraction and smallest-label fraction. e, Expression sparsity plotted as zero fraction against mean detected highly variable genes per cell; point size denotes cells. f, Cumulative PCA variance at PC5, PC10 and PC20 for each dataset. g, PC-based label separability, summarized by PC-kNN recall and PC20 silhouette score. h, Detection-depth spread; points show medians and whiskers show the 10th to 90th percentile range of detected highly variable genes per cell. i, Library-size depth summarized by median and mean total counts per cell. j, Rare-depth burden, combining rare-cell fraction, rare-label fraction and scaled median detected genes. Panels are empirical-derived dataset summaries, not method-performance results. n denotes dataset rows, label rows, donor rows or cell-level summaries as specified by the corresponding source table. Numerical values and provenance are indexed in Supplementary Tables 1-3, 5 and 11.

**Supplementary Fig. S2 | Metric-level summary of the eight-method mechanism simulation suite.** a-f, Controlled known-truth simulation scenarios used to stress-test claim-specific vulnerabilities without converting the review into a new large-scale benchmark. Panels show linear low-rank structure, nonlinear manifold structure, branching trajectory, dropout stress, batch shift and rare population scenarios. Rows are the fixed eight-method representative panel, PCA, GLM-PCA, scScope, SAUCIE, UMAP, PHATE, t-SNE and PaCMAP. Columns show scenario-relevant diagnostics, including local retention against known truth, trustworthiness, latent-distance correlation, label-neighbour recall, pseudotime-distance correlation, batch entropy and rare-label recall. Only metrics that were defined and calculated for a scenario are displayed. Colour encodes the ratio of the observed metric to the prespecified threshold, capped for display at 1.6. In-cell letters denote pass (P) or below-threshold failure (F). This figure is simulation-derived evidence for mechanism stress testing and does not represent new empirical biology. Simulation parameters, thresholds, completion status and scenario-level summaries are indexed in Supplementary Tables 8-10, with source-file provenance in Supplementary Table 11.

**Supplementary Fig. S3 | Full embedding atlas across empirical interpretation contexts.** a-c, Low-dimensional coordinates for the complete eight-method representative panel in PBMC3k, Paul15 and the heart-atlas subset. Columns show PCA, GLM-PCA, scScope, SAUCIE, UMAP, PHATE, t-SNE and PaCMAP, ordered by mechanism family. Points are cells and colours denote the dataset-specific annotation labels shown at the right of each row. The panels provide the visual atlas underlying the matched comparisons in the main text, but visual separation is not treated as evidence by itself. The corresponding diagnostic and biological-support decisions are quantified in Figs. 3-5 and indexed in Supplementary Tables 5 and 11. n denotes cells in the processed analysis object for each dataset-method map.

**Supplementary Fig. S4 | Diagnostic-gate support, component ratios and threshold margins.** a, Method-level support fractions for label, local-neighbourhood, global-geometry, continuum and donor-aware diagnostic gates. b, Mechanism-family support fractions for the same gates, computed from the two representative methods in each family. c, Local-gate component ratios, defined as observed value divided by the prespecified threshold for local retention, trustworthiness and label-neighbour recall. d, Global-rank preservation ratios across PBMC3k, Paul15 and the heart-atlas subset. e, Paul15 continuum-gate ratios for pseudotime-neighbourhood retention and pseudotime-rank correlation. f, Heart-atlas donor-aware ratios for cell-type recall and donor-entropy support. g, Family-level threshold margins, calculated as diagnostic value minus threshold. h, Within-family gate spread, showing the range between the lowest and highest method pass fractions within each mechanism family. Heatmap colours encode support fraction, value-to-threshold ratio or threshold margin as indicated by each colour bar. Rows represent method, family or dataset-metric units rather than independent biological replicates; full gate definitions and source provenance are indexed in Supplementary Tables 5, 6 and 11.

**Supplementary Fig. S5 | Independent biological anchors for embedding-based claims.** a, PBMC3k marker-program support by annotated cell type, summarized as the maximum marker-program z-score. b, PBMC3k label-neighbour recall by method and label. c, Family-level dual support for PBMC3k labels, requiring both marker-program support and embedding-neighbour recovery. d, Paul15 lineage-anchor trends, shown as Spearman correlations between marker-program score and pseudotime; dashed lines mark the prespecified absolute-correlation criterion. e, Heart-atlas identity versus donor-mixing support, with x-axis showing cell-type recall and y-axis showing normalized donor entropy; dashed lines mark the donor-aware support thresholds. f, Rare-label recall by method across PBMC3k and heart-atlas rare states. g, Rare-state support fraction by mechanism family and dataset. h, Integrated independent-anchor support fractions across discrete identity, rare-state, donor-variable tissue and continuum claims. These panels use biological anchors external to the plotted embedding geometry, including marker programs, pseudotime-associated marker trends and donor-aware neighbourhood summaries. n denotes method-label, method-rare-label, lineage-program or method-level rows as defined in Supplementary Tables 5 and 11.

**Supplementary Fig. S6 | Robustness response across dimensionality, upstream representation and perturbation.** a, Mean diagnostic response across output dimensions for local retention, trustworthiness, global-rank preservation and label-neighbour recall. b, Failure fractions under output-dimension variation by dataset and metric. c, Failure fractions under output-dimension variation by recomputed method and metric. d, Mean diagnostic response across upstream PCA dimensions for graph and relational methods. e, Failure fractions under upstream-PCA variation by method and metric. f, Mean diagnostic response under dropout and noise perturbations; solid lines denote dropout and dashed lines denote noise. g, Perturbation failure fraction by metric and perturbation type. h, Worst-case support ratio for the recomputable robustness subset, calculated as the minimum observed value divided by the prespecified threshold across predeclared conditions. i, Worst-case pass and below-threshold burden by method for the same robustness subset. Panels summarize robustness and sensitivity analyses rather than independent biological replicates; thresholds, axes, availability and source provenance are indexed in Supplementary Tables 6, 7, 11 and 14.
